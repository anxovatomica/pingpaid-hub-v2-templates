Client Referral Request Templates

Turn happy clients into leads · Updated June 2026 · Copy and paste

Referrals are the highest-quality leads you can get. A happy client introducing you is worth 10 cold emails. Here's how to ask.

📧 The Direct Referral Ask

Subject: Quick question — know anyone who needs [service]?

Hi [Client Name],

I'm so glad we were able to [result from project]. Your [specific thing] was a great project to work on.

I'm currently looking to take on [X] more clients in the [timeframe]. Do you know anyone who might need [service]?

I'm happy to offer them the same quality of work we delivered for you. If someone comes to mind, I'd really appreciate the introduction.

Thanks so much!

Best regards,
[Your Name]

📧 The Incentive-Based Referral

Subject: Referral bonus — $500 for every new client

Hi [Client Name],

I'm excited to share that I've launched a referral program for past clients like you.

For every new client you refer who books a project, I'll send you a $500 thank-you bonus after their first payment.

There's no limit — refer 3 clients, get $1,500. And of course, the new client gets priority scheduling and my full attention.

Know anyone who might benefit from [service]? Just reply with their name or make an intro.

Thanks for being such a great client!

Best regards,
[Your Name]

📧 The LinkedIn Introduction

Subject: LinkedIn intro request

Hi [Client Name],

I'm expanding my client base and looking for companies in [industry/niche] who need [service].

Would you be comfortable making a LinkedIn introduction to anyone in your network who might need help? I promise to make you look good by delivering excellent work.

Happy to send you a blurb you can copy/paste if that makes it easier.

Thanks so much!

Best regards,
[Your Name]

📧 The Post-Project Referral

Subject: Thanks for the project — one last question

Hi [Client Name],

[Project Name] is complete and I'm so happy with how it turned out. I hope you're seeing [specific result] already.

One last question: do you know anyone in your network who might benefit from similar work? A warm introduction from you would mean the world.

If someone comes to mind, just reply with their email and I'll reach out. No pressure at all — I appreciate you either way!

Best regards,
[Your Name]

💡 Pro tip: Ask for referrals when the client is happiest — right after a successful project, after receiving positive feedback, or after delivering above expectations. Never ask during a difficult phase or when they're unhappy.
