Client Feedback Request Templates

Build social proof · Updated June 2026 · Copy and paste

Testimonials are the #1 trust signal for new clients. Here's how to ask for them without being awkward.

📧 The Direct Testimonial Request

Subject: Quick favor — a testimonial for [Project Name]?

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you're still happy with [Project Name]! I'm working on building my portfolio and would love to feature our work together.

Would you be willing to write a brief testimonial? Just 2-3 sentences about what it was like working with me. Here's a template if you need it:

"[Your Name] was [adjective] to work with. They [specific result]. I would [recommend/strongly recommend] them to anyone looking for [service]."

Or if you prefer, just answer these three questions:

1. What was the biggest challenge before we started?
2. What specific result did we achieve?
3. What would you say to someone considering hiring me?

Thanks so much!

[Your Name]

📧 The Feedback + Testimonial Combo

Subject: How did [Project Name] go? + Quick favor

Hi [Client Name],

Now that [Project Name] has wrapped up, I'd love to hear how it went from your perspective.

Quick 2-minute survey (literally 2 minutes):

1. On a scale of 1-10, how satisfied are you with the result?
2. What was the best part of working together?
3. Is there anything I could have done better?
4. Would you be willing to write a brief testimonial? (Just reply to this email with 2-3 sentences)

Your feedback helps me improve, and the testimonial helps other clients find me. Win-win!

Thanks,
[Your Name]

📧 The LinkedIn Recommendation Request

Subject: LinkedIn recommendation?

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you're doing well! I wanted to ask if you'd be willing to write a LinkedIn recommendation for me.

It takes 2 minutes and helps me build credibility. If you're happy to do it, here's a link: [LinkedIn recommendation URL]

Or if you prefer, just reply with a few sentences and I'll format it.

Thanks so much!

[Your Name]

💡 Pro tip: Ask for testimonials immediately after a successful project. The client is happy. The work is fresh. The testimonial is specific. Wait 3 months and they'll forget the details.
